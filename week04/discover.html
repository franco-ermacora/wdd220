BIG CITY

Rosario is the third largest city in Argentina, it has everything one needs, beautiful places to visit, good food, lots of people to meet, the only problem is insecurity, being the most dangerous in all of Argentina.

PARK SPAIN

The España park is the largest park in Rosario, since it borders the entire part of the Parana River (one of the largest in Latin America), where there are all kinds of recreational, cultural and sports activities.

PARANÁ RIVER

Rosario is a city that overlooks the river and there are many options to be in contact with nature. The imposing Paraná River borders the city and can be enjoyed both from the large green spaces of the parks on the central bank and from the beaches of the northern zone with wide stretches of sand. In addition, you can do different activities such as riding a boat, rubber boat or sailboat, rowing in a kayak or SUP or go on a fishing excursion. Crossing the river and reaching the islands is another option to enjoy the beaches in a few minutes and have a wonderful view of the city's silhouette.

PARK INDEPENDCE

The Parque de la Independencia was the first park in the city, inaugurated on January 1, 1902. Its origin is linked to four public squares at the intersection of two new wide boulevards, the "Bv. Santafesino" and the "Bv. Argentino "(current Bv. Oroño and Av. Pellegrini). The four squares were called "Independence".

AQUARIUM

The Paraná River Aquarium, formally the Paraná River Aquarium Scientific, Technological and Educational Center, is a public aquarium that was built to be a scientific and educational establishment. It is located in the city of Rosario, in the Argentine province of Santa Fe.
